Built for Montana

Dry winters are hard on hardwood. We know what survives them.

Montana's forced-air heating pulls indoor humidity to 20% or lower from October through April. Most wood floors aren't laid with that in mind. Ours are.

Chris selects species and finishes for this specific climate — white oak and hickory for stability, water-based finishes that flex with seasonal movement and cure fast in low-humidity conditions.

That's not a product recommendation. It's 18 years of watching what fails here and choosing accordingly.

About Chris Traver

Six generations of Montana roots. 18 years of hardwood floors.

Chris grew up in Bozeman — a sixth-generation Montana native who spent 18 years doing one thing across western Montana: hardwood floors. In 2023, he relocated to Helena for the housing stock — block after block of turn-of-the-century homes with original fir and pine buried under decades of carpet.

He's mentored four sons in the trade. The Traver name goes on every floor we lay, and that means something to us.
